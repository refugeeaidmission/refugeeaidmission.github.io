Our mission is to mobilize the Church.

We work with displaced people and refugees in the Middle East to meet their needs and share the Gospel with them.

Our Strategy

  • Be a consistent presence to refugees and displaced people in the Middle East.
  • Connect churches in the United States with particular refugee camps and/or refugee families and displaced people.
  • Connect refugees and displaced people with local congregations in the Middle East.
  • Share Biblical principles to strengthen the faith of refugees and displaced people.

About Refugee Aid Mission

Founders—Terry and Melissa Rhodes

Terry and Melissa worked in the Middle East in education for seven years. Terry served as a high school principal and helped to open a new school in the area. Melissa taught junior high, high school, and preschool age children. In addition to school activities they had a robust ministry with students, their families, and in the community. They initiated an afterschool program for high school students featuring tutoring, Bible studies, worship, coffee shop, retreats, seminars, fun activities, lots of food, and community outreach projects.

For the past three years they have led short term trips to the Middle East partnering with local pastors and missionaries to engage in refugee outreach. They are relocating to the Middle East to be able to host those interested in refugee outreach and to continue to be a presence there.

Local Director—Heba Rabi

Heba Rabi was born and raised in Bethlehem. She graduated from the University of Arkansas in 2017. She worked for AIA as a student intern and later worked for AIA full time as a disciple maker. Her desire is to follow the Lord’s lead and share His love by pointing others to Him and encouraging others to read God’s word daily. She has relocated to the Middle East and works with RAM hosting short term teams and assisting in refugee outreach.

Advisors—Jayne and Rick Brekelbaum

Jayne and Rick Brekelbaum have had a full life serving the Lord. Their retirement has been a re-hirement by the Lord. They are active in their church community, attending conferences, teaching/mentoring for CEO Global, training for ION, serving on the board of Pure Charity, creating evangelical tools, hosting many small groups, and more. They currently live in Northwest Arkansas and serve RAM as advisors in addition to their many hats.


Stories from the Middle East

Deported: Nancy's Story

Nancy has two babies, 1 year old and 3 years old. Her husband was deported last year because the government is overloaded with refugees due to all the conflict in the Middle East.

Read more

The government is trying to find ways of dealing with the crisis. Nancy and her husband fled due to conflict in their country and after her husband’s deportation she is living alone in the Middle East with no family and no income. Her husband was able to contact her and told her he is trying to find a way to reunite the family. He may try to come back into the Middle East.

This woman is living in a run-down studio apartment in a slum area. She does her best to care for her babies and hopes that someday she can live in Canada or America with her family.

We visited her with a missionary in the Middle East, and during the visit the missionary gave her testimony of how Jesus changed her life. Nancy called the missionary the day after our visit and said how much that visit encouraged her and how happy she was that someone cared for her. It was a cold cup of water, a small act of compassion, in Jesus’ name.

How will this money be used?

We raise money to help with Bible studies, schools, and other ministries in the Middle East who help refugees.

With your help, we will be able to help support the missionaries and pastors who continually give to and work with the refugees. We will be able to give necessities to the refugees we visit. We thank you for your support enabling us to give to those in need.

Refugee Aid Mission is an affiliate of Mission Enablers International (MEI), which is a registered 501c3 in the state of Arkansas. All donations will receive a tax receipt and are eligible for deduction. Donations of any size are accepted. Your donations will go to help refugees in the Middle East.
